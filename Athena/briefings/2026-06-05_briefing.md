# MedTech Intelligence Briefing - June 05, 2026

## Breaking

GE HealthCare issued an [FDA Early Alert](http://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/medical-device-recalls-and-early-alerts/early-alert-infant-resuscitation-system-issue-ge-healthcare) after the air-oxygen blender knob shaft was found loosening on integrated and stand-alone infant resuscitation units, compromising delivered oxygen concentration — a safety signal on life-support equipment that frequently precedes a Class I recall.

## Need to Know

**GE HealthCare neonatal resuscitation — Early Alert, not yet a full recall.** A loose blender knob can mis-set oxygen concentration on neonatal/pediatric life support. For a QA/RA leader this is a textbook design-control and post-market-surveillance failure: clients building respiratory or neonatal devices should audit comparable shaft-locking mechanisms now, before the [Early Alert](http://www.fda.gov/medical-devices/medical-device-recalls-and-early-alerts/early-alert-infant-resuscitation-system-issue-ge-healthcare) escalates.
