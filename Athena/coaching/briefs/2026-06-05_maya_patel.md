## What We Know

Maya Patel, Regulatory Affairs Specialist at Edwards Lifesciences (Irvine, CA). Five years in the industry — approximately two at a smaller San Diego startup (Class II cardiovascular devices) then three at Edwards. Current scope: 510(k) preparation, EU MDR technical file support, and post-market surveillance reporting. Reached out after attending a Biocom SD chapter panel where Latitude MedTech was mentioned. Indicated in her inquiry email that she is "ready for the next level but keeps getting passed over for manager roles."

## Likely Profile

Strong technical foundation — someone who can run a submission but is hitting the classic mid-career ceiling where technical competence alone doesn't get you promoted. At Edwards, RA Manager roles are competitive and typically require demonstrated cross-functional leadership, not just submission quality. The "passed over" signal is important: this is almost certainly a visibility/executive presence problem, not a knowledge gap. She likely knows the regulations well.

Secondary consideration: EU MDR exposure at her level is genuinely valuable and differentiating — this is a coaching lever. If she's been working on MDR tech files at Edwards, she's ahead of most RA Specialists in the market.

## Recommended Program Match

**Primary: Mid-Career Acceleration ($1,899 / 3 months)**
Clear fit. The focus on executive presence, salary negotiation, and leadership positioning is precisely what separates RA Specialists who stay stuck from those who get the RM or Director offer. Three months is the right timeframe to get her ready for the next internal cycle or an external move.

**Secondary: consider positioning as pre-search coaching** — if Maya is open to leaving Edwards, the program can be tailored to make her externally competitive for RA Manager roles at SoCal startups, where her OEM background is premium.

## Discovery Call Objectives

1. Understand the "passed over" story — was it once, twice, same role? What feedback did she get, if any?
2. Establish whether she wants to grow within Edwards or move: different strategies entirely.
3. Assess executive presence gaps — does she present to senior leadership? Is she on any cross-functional teams? What does her relationship with the VP RA look like?
4. Clarify EU MDR depth — is she the lead on tech files, or supporting someone else?

## Suggested Opening Questions

1. "You mentioned being passed over for manager roles — can you walk me through the most recent situation? What role was it, and what was the feedback you received?"
2. "When you picture yourself as an RA Manager 18 months from now, is that at Edwards specifically, or are you open to it being somewhere else?"
3. "Do you present directly to senior stakeholders at Edwards — VPs, cross-functional leads — or is most of your work file-based?"
4. "You've been working on EU MDR at Edwards — are you leading those tech file updates or supporting a senior RA person?"
5. "What would it mean for you if you got the manager title? Is it the comp, the scope, or something else?"

## Watch-Outs

- If the "passed over" story involves a specific internal political dynamic at Edwards (a manager blocking her, a rival candidate), coaching can help with framing but can't fix org politics — be honest about that scope limit.
- Maya may be more ready than she thinks and may benefit more from an external job search strategy than an extended coaching program. If the first call reveals strong fundamentals, be direct about this rather than over-scoping.
- Five years with an Edwards stint is a very strong resume. Don't let her undervalue what she's bringing — the coaching framing should be amplification, not remediation.
