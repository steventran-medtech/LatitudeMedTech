## The Real Mechanics

The International Medical Device Regulators Forum (IMDRF) — the global body that harmonizes regulatory frameworks across FDA, Health Canada, the EU, TGA, and others — officially consolidated its adverse event reporting annexes in May 2025. If you've been tracking IMDRF's Medical Device Problem Codes work, you know these annexes have been the backbone of post-market surveillance (monitoring a device's safety after it reaches patients) reporting for over a decade.

Here's what changed and why your org chart is catching up:

**Annex D** (Cause Investigation – Investigation Conclusion) defines how root cause investigations must be structured and concluded. Traditionally, this lived inside QA's CAPA process. Under the consolidated framework, that conclusion language now has to interface directly with Annex E and F clinical language — meaning your cause investigation can't be closed in isolation from clinical signs data.

**Annex E** (Health Effects – Clinical Signs and Symptoms or Conditions) and **Annex F** (Health Effects – Health Impact) now form an integrated clinical assessment pair. These used to operate as adjacent references. Post-consolidation, they're effectively co-dependent: you can't complete a compliant health effects evaluation without mapping symptoms to impact in a single documented thread. This is work that previously crossed the QA/RA handoff point — and now that seam doesn't exist.

**Annex G** (Medical Device Component) tightens the component-level reporting requirements in ways that pull manufacturing QA engineers into regulatory submissions conversations they've never been part of before.
