## CONTENT TO PUBLISH — SUBSTACK · MedTech Meridian

**Proposed title:** *"Your AI Regulatory Consultant Can't Hear You — And That's a $500K Problem"*

**Contrarian claim to anchor the post:**
> The Big 3 AI regulatory platforms (Lilli, GENE, and their peers) are document retrieval engines dressed as advisors. They return citations. They do not reason through your specific device classification, your predicate selection risk, or your FDA reviewer's documented history with your product code. Voice-first, interactive reasoning isn't a UX preference — it's the difference between a regulatory strategy and a regulatory bibliography.

**Structure (500–700 words, no fluff):**
1. One concrete scenario: a De Novo applicant who got a citation dump instead of a Special Controls recommendation
2. What "interactive reasoning" actually means in a 510(k) context (50-year knowledge base applied, not retrieved)
3. Three questions your AI tool should be able to answer live — and what it means if it can't
4. CTA: Link to Zoom Regulatory Clinic sign-up (no hard sell — "bring your one hardest question")
