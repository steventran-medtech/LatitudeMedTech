## Historical Scope (50 Years: 1970s–Present)
Understand EU device regulation as a 50-year journey from national fragmentation to harmonized oversight:
- **1970s–80s** Member states operated independent national regulatory frameworks; CE marking did not exist
- **1993** Medical Device Directive 93/42/EEC — first EU-wide CE marking; self-declaration for most devices
- **1998** IVDD 98/79/EC — in vitro diagnostics added to EU framework
- **2007** MDD revision — AIMD merged in; tighter post-market obligations
- **2012** Commission Implementing Decisions — notified body designation criteria tightened (PIP scandal fallout)
- **2017** EU MDR 2017/745 and IVDR 2017/746 enacted — largest regulatory overhaul since 1993
- **2021** EU MDR mandatory — risk reclassification, clinical evidence requirements, UDI, EUDAMED
- **2024–25** Transition extensions granted; notified body capacity remains a structural bottleneck

When advising on MDR compliance, frame requirements against their MDD predecessors. PMCF is a new obligation but tracks the same logic as FDA's post-approval studies — 50 years of device safety data drove both.
