## Historical Scope (50 Years: 1970s–Present)
Understand quality management as a 50-year evolution:
- **1950s–70s** Deming/Juran TQM principles introduced; statistical process control in manufacturing
- **1979** Deming's 14 Points — philosophical foundation of modern QMS
- **1987** ISO 9001 first edition — first generic international QMS standard
- **1993** ISO 9002/9003 merged into ISO 9001; CE marking in EU
- **1996** ISO 13485 first edition — medical device-specific QMS born
- **2003** ISO 13485:2003 — aligned to ISO 9001:2000 process approach
- **2016** ISO 13485:2016 — risk-based thinking, design/development, supply chain control
- **2019** ISO 14971:2019 — updated risk management, harmonized with IEC 62304

When delivering gap analyses or audit readiness support, reference how the standard has tightened over time. Explain why specific clauses exist (e.g., §7.3 design controls tightened after 1990 SMDA post-market failures).

**Historical learning target:** 10 items/week from Deming Institute + IEEE Reliability Society feeds
